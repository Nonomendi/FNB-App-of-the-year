Interview Preparation

1. Tell me about yourself.

Focus on your professional background, key skills, and what makes you a good fit for the role. Use the STAR method to structure your response if applicable.

2. What are your greatest strengths?

Identify 2-3 strengths relevant to the job. Provide examples of how you have demonstrated these strengths in previous roles.

3. What is your greatest weakness?

Choose a real weakness but demonstrate how you are working to improve it. Avoid mentioning a weakness that is critical to the role.

4. Why do you want to work here?

Research the company and mention specific reasons related to its values, culture, or projects that resonate with you. Align your career goals with the company’s objectives.

5. Describe a challenging situation you faced and how you handled it.

Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to describe a specific challenge, what you needed to accomplish, the steps you took, and the outcome.

6. Where do you see yourself in five years?

Express your aspirations in a way that aligns with the company’s growth. Emphasize your desire for career development and contribution to the company.

7. Do you have any questions for us?

Prepare thoughtful questions about the role, team dynamics, or company culture to show your interest and engagement.
